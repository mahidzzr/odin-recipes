Milk Egg Caramel Pudding

Description

Milk Egg Caramel Pudding, also known as crème caramel or flan, is a classic dessert characterized by its smooth, creamy texture and rich caramel flavor. The dessert features a layer of golden, sweet caramel sauce atop a soft and silky custard base made from milk, eggs, and sugar. The custard is baked in a water bath to ensure even cooking and a delicate, jiggly consistency. When served, the pudding is inverted onto a plate, allowing the caramel to flow over the custard, creating a delightful contrast between the creamy custard and the sweet, slightly bitter caramel.

Ingredients

  • For the Caramel:
    • 1 cup (200g) sugar
    • 1/4 cup (60ml) water
  • For the Custard:
    • 2 cups (480ml) whole milk
    • 4 large eggs
    • 1/2 cup(100g) suger
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)

Steps

  1. For the Caramel:
    1. Combine 1 cup of sugar with 1/4 cup of water in a saucepan.
    2. Heat until the sugar dissolves and turns amber.
    3. Pour the caramel into the bottom of a baking dish or ramekins.
  2. For the Custard:
    1. Warm 2 cups of whole milk until just about to simmer.
    2. Whisk together 4 large eggs and 1/2 cup of sugar.
    3. Add 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract (optional).
    4. Gradually whisk the warm milk into the egg mixture.
    5. Strain the custard mixture to remove any solids.
  3. Baking:
    1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (160°C).
    2. Pour the custard mixture over the caramel in the baking dish.
    3. Place the dish in a larger pan filled with hot water halfway up the sides.
    4. Bake for 45-60 minutes until the custard is set but slightly jiggly.
  4. Finishing
    1. Cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for at least 2 hours.
    2. Invert onto a plate to serve.
