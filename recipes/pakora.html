Vegetable Pakora

Description

Pakora is a popular South Asian snack made by deep-frying vegetables, chicken, or other ingredients that are dipped in a seasoned gram flour (besan) batter. These crispy fritters are enjoyed across India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, and other parts of South Asia.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1/4 cup rice flour (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon ajwain (carom seeds)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 1-2 potatoes, thinly sliced or grated
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables finely chopped
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • Oil for deep frying

Steps

  1. Mix gram flour, rice flour, cumin seeds, ajwain, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt in a bowl.
  2. Gradually add water to make a smooth, thick batter.
  3. Stir in green chilies, onion, potatoes, mixed vegetables, and coriander leaves.
  4. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
  5. Drop small portions of the batter into hot oil.
  6. Fry until golden brown and crispy, about 4-6 minutes per batch.
  7. Drain on paper towels.
  8. Serve hot with green chutney, tamarind chutney, or ketchup. Enjoy!
